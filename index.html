🐦 Birdwatching & hiking guide
Costa Rica birdwatching context: With over 850 identified bird species, Costa Rica has more bird species per square mile than any country in North America. The Papagayo Peninsula and Guanacaste region alone support 250+ species across dry forest, mangrove, wetland, and coastal ecosystems — all within 1–2 hours of the Waldorf. July is green season, which means lush vegetation, fewer crowds on trails, and active breeding behavior. The trade-off vs. dry season is that some migratory species are absent, but resident species — including the most dramatic ones — are very present. Bring binoculars. Even a modest pair transforms the experience.
Birds to look for in Guanacaste, July: Turquoise-browed Motmot (the iconic "pendulum bird" of Costa Rica) · Long-tailed Manakin (performs elaborate synchronized courtship dances) · Orange-fronted Parakeet · Crested Caracara · Laughing Falcon · Squirrel Cuckoo · Elegant Trogon · Spot-breasted Oriole · White-fronted Parrot · Spectacled Owl · Clay-colored Thrush (Costa Rica's national bird) · Roseate Spoonbill (Palo Verde) · Great Egret · Wood Stork · Anhinga · Yellow-crowned Night-Heron · Scarlet Macaw · Northern Jacana

🌅 Horizontes Forest Private Reserve — Dawn Chorus Tour
~20 min from resort · Departs 5:10am · 3 hours · Max 8 people · Easy
A private farm reserve within the Guanacaste Conservation Area — once cattle ranching land, now a restored mosaic of tropical dry forest, wetlands, open savanna, and reforested corridors that attract both resident and migratory species. The tour starts at the reserve then walks down to beach areas to spot shorebirds. An expert naturalist guide carries a high-powered spotting scope and field reference books. Key species: Long-tailed Manakins (performing their famous synchronized dances), Turquoise-browed Motmot, Crested Caracara, Laughing Falcon, Squirrel Cuckoo, Orange-fronted Parakeet. Breakfast is included mid-tour. Groups never exceed 8 people. The dawn chorus — every resident bird singing at full volume as the forest wakes up — is something neither of you will forget.
Departs 5:10am — early start ~$55–$75/person Book: thecostaricatoursite.com or via concierge Transport from Waldorf included

🦩 Palo Verde National Park — Tempisque River Safari
~90 min from resort · Departs 7am · Returns ~2pm · Half-day boat safari
Costa Rica's most important wetland sanctuary — a Ramsar Wetland of International Importance and UNESCO Biosphere Reserve protecting 45,000 acres of tropical dry forest. The experience is a leisurely 1.5–2 hour covered motorboat cruise along the Tempisque River, the longest river in Guanacaste. Wildlife sightings are essentially guaranteed: howler monkeys, green iguanas, and American crocodiles are virtually always seen. For birdwatchers, the centrepiece is "Isla de Pájaros" (Bird Island) — the primary nesting colony at the heart of the river where dozens of species roost simultaneously: Roseate Spoonbills, Great Egrets, Wood Storks, Anhingas, Black-crowned Night-Herons, White Ibis, Green-backed Herons, Yellow-crowned Night-Herons. In green season the vegetation is lush and bird breeding behavior is active. Baby crocodiles are a July bonus. Organized tours from the Papagayo/Waldorf area include round-trip transportation, bilingual naturalist guide, park entrance, 2-hour boat safari, and traditional Costa Rican lunch — a full experience. Tours depart directly from the Waldorf Astoria Punta Cacique.
Departs from Waldorf — no separate transfer ~$110–$160/person organized tour Includes transport, guide, park fee, lunch Book via papagayotours.com or concierge 279 bird species in the park · 50,000+ waterfowl

🥾 Las Catalinas Coastal Trail Loop
~20 min from resort · 4 miles · ~2 hrs · Easy-moderate · Self-guided
Las Catalinas is a beautifully maintained pedestrian village with a network of well-marked trails through dry tropical forest running down to the Pacific coast. The Las Cruces–Punta Guachipelines loop (4 miles, about 2 hours) passes through forest canopy with great birding for dry forest specialists, then opens to dramatic coastal cliffs. Trail is well-maintained, shaded in parts, and muddy in spots during green season — bring good walking shoes. No entrance fee, no guide required, can be reached by taxi or rental car from the Waldorf. Ideal for a relaxed early morning walk before beach time.
Free · Self-guided · AllTrails listed Wear closed-toe shoes · Can be muddy in July ~$15 taxi each way from Waldorf
Option 5 — Casual, closest option (~15 min)
Optional — any morning, no commitment
🦅 Playa Hermosa Wildlife Refuge — Mangrove Walk
~15 min from resort · Self-guided or guided · 45–90 min · Easy
The closest birdwatching site to the Waldorf. The Playa Hermosa Wildlife Refuge encompasses beach, mangrove estuary, and coastal forest ecosystems — producing a different and complementary species list to the dry forest interior. Shorebirds, wading birds, and waterfowl are the specialty: Tricolored Herons, Great Blue Herons, Snowy Egrets, Black-necked Stilts, Willets, and migratory sandpipers. The mangrove fringe at low tide is particularly productive. Best visited early morning. A local guide from the Hermosa beach community (~$30–40/person) adds significant value for identification. Alternatively walk independently at sunrise before heading back to the resort for breakfast.
